Computer science research-y

  • (2019) (Planned submission to ACL 2020) I'm currently working on my undergraduate thesis with Prof. Ellie Pavlick, tentatively titled “When do neural models learn heuristics?” We're studying (empirically for now) the tipping point when models switch from (1) learning simple rules and memorizing exceptions to those rules, to (2) learning more complex and generalizable rules. I received an Undergraduate Thesis and Research Award (UTRA) from Brown to work on this project. We submitted an extended abstract (here) to the Natural Language, Dialog and Speech (NDS) Symposium at the New York Academy of Sciences. An in-progress repository is here.

  • (2019) I'm working with Profs. Ellie Pavlick and Roman Feiman on simulating implicature-like phenomena with neural networks (including BERT). We're interested in whether neural networks can predict the alternatives that listeners generate in the Rational Speech Acts (RSA) model. An in-progress repository with these models is here.

  • (2018-2019) As part of a graduate algorithms seminar with Prof. Paul Valiant, I presented “Probabilistic encryption” by Goldwasser and Micali (with Miranda Christ and Noah Picard) and “Undirected connectivity in log-space” by Reingold (with Miranda Christ and Shivam Nadimpalli) (slides here). Miranda Christ and I also wrote summaries of “Hardness vs. randomness” by Nisan and Wigderson (here) and Dinur's proof of the PCP theorem (here), and we previously presented “Primes in P” by Agarwal, Kayal, and Saxena.

  • (2018) Varun Mathur and I built a model (code here) and wrote a short paper (here) for the EmoContext task at SemEval 2019, as part of a graduate course with Prof. Ellie Pavlick.
